Reading

I didn't read much as a kid, but started to read a lot more in college. I usually alternate between fiction and non-fiction. Often, I am eager to leave lab or skip hanging out with friends to finish the book I'm reading.

My favorite fiction genre, when I can get through it, is 20th century modernist literature. James Joyce's Ulysses was incredible, although I only understood 10% of it even with the help of a professor. As for non-fiction, I like to read popular science books, especially about the history of technology, but not often about biology because I do research in that field.

Right now, I'm reading the short story anthology Writer's Choice assembled by L. R. Hill. Each of about 20 American authors chose their favorite story to include. I particularly liked Capote's "Children on Their Birthdays" and Yates' "A Really Good Jazz Piano."

Next, I look forward to reading A Yiddish Policemen's Union by Michael Chabon.
